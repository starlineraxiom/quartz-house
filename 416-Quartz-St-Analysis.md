## 3. Flags & diligence items from the inspection record

Nothing alarming, but concrete things to verify:

1. **It's a developer/investor renovation, not a long-term owner's home.** The permit
   was pulled in the seller's name and the house was listed within days of final.
   Scrutinize finish quality and ask who the GC and structural engineer were.
2. **Neighbor friction during construction:**
   - 4/6/2026 — **dust complaint** from a neighbor over stone-cutting; inspector
     intervened (told crew to wet-cut / move into garage / wear masks).
   - 7/15/2025 — **safety correction** for failure to install trench shoring (≥5 ft trenches).
   Both resolved, but they indicate an aggressive job site; a courtesy word with
   neighbors is wise.
3. **Final-inspection punch list (5/22/2026), all later cleared 6/5/2026** — gives a
   read on build quality. Items included: missing stair handrails, UV insulation on
   exposed outdoor water-heater lines, bathroom-fan humidity sensors, a GFCI/cover at
   the attic furnace, intersystem bonding at the panel, water-heater jumper bond, and
   a too-temporary (raw 2×4) support penetrating the **garage firewall** for the
   ejector-pump drain. These are normal final-pass corrections — verify the ejector-pump
   firewall fix was done permanently.
4. **Basement + ejector pump** — the addition includes finished basement space served
   by a sewage **ejector pump** (mechanical, can fail/needs maintenance) and the lot
   required 13 piers. Suggests a sloped/hillside Eagle Hill lot with some soils/drainage
   considerations. Get a geotech/soils read if available.
5. **The exterior water heater** is outdoors (lines needed UV insulation) — confirm
   it's tankless and properly freeze/weather protected.

## 6. Walkability

Eagle Hill is a quiet, hilly, almost entirely residential pocket — pleasant to walk *in*, but
you drive to most errands. **Walk Score ≈ 55 ("Somewhat Walkable")** for neighboring 434 Quartz St.

**Walkable from the door**
- **Quartz St & Avenue Del Ora parklet** — a small triangular green right where the street splits,
  essentially at the doorstep.
- **John Gill Elementary School** — 7-acre campus a few blocks away; local public schools rate
  above average.
- **Three more neighborhood parklets** — Alameda de las Pulgas & Hopkins; Brewster & Hillview; and
  the largest, **Laurie Duncan Parkway** at Brewster & Nevada (picnic benches).
- Quiet, low-traffic streets that follow the hill — good for strolls and dog walks.

**A longer walk / short drive**
- **Red Morton Community Park** (~1 mi) — all-abilities **Magical Bridge Playground**, skate park,
  soccer fields, basketball, Dove pool.
- **Downtown Redwood City** (~1 mi, ~20 min walk) — Courthouse Square, restaurants, cinema,
  farmers' market, Caltrain. Downhill out, uphill back.
- **Sequoia High School** & the El Camino corridor for everyday services.
- **Groceries:** none in-neighborhood — nearest supermarkets are a short drive toward downtown /
  Woodside Plaza.

**Bottom line:** charming for walks, parklets, and the elementary school, and downtown + a major
playground are within a (hilly) mile — but with no stores in the neighborhood and steep streets,
it's car-oriented for groceries and daily errands. Typical of a hillside Peninsula enclave.

*Sources: Walking Redwood City (Eagle Hill), Walk Score (434 Quartz St), Redwood City Parks.*
