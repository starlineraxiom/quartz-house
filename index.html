3Diligence & watch-items

Nothing alarming, but concrete things to verify.

  • Investor/developer renovation, not a long-term owner's home. Listed within days of final. Scrutinize finish quality; ask who the GC and structural engineer were and whether there's a workmanship warranty.
  • Construction-period neighbor friction: a dust complaint over stone-cutting (Apr 2026) and a trench-shoring safety correction (Jul 2025). Both resolved, but they point to an aggressive job site.
  • Final punch list (5/22/2026), cleared 6/5/2026: missing stair handrails, UV insulation on outdoor water-heater lines, bath-fan humidity sensors, attic-furnace GFCI, intersystem bonding, WH jumper bond, and a too-temporary support penetrating the garage firewall for the ejector-pump drain. Verify the firewall fix is permanent.
  • Basement + sewage ejector pump (mechanical, needs maintenance) and a 13-pier foundation suggest a sloped lot with soils/drainage considerations — ask for a geotech report.
  • Outdoor water heater (lines needed UV insulation) — confirm it's tankless and properly weather/freeze protected.

Walkable from the door

  • Quartz St & Avenue Del Ora parklet — a small triangular green right where the street splits, essentially at the doorstep.
  • John Gill Elementary School — 7-acre campus a few blocks away; local public schools rate above average.
  • Three more neighborhood parklets — Alameda de las Pulgas & Hopkins; Brewster & Hillview; and the largest, Laurie Duncan Parkway at Brewster & Nevada (picnic benches).
  • Quiet, low-traffic streets — good for strolls and dog walks.

A longer walk / short drive

  • Red Morton Community Park (~1 mi) — all-abilities Magical Bridge Playground, skate park, soccer fields, basketball, Dove pool.
  • Downtown Redwood City (~1 mi, ~20 min walk) — Courthouse Square, restaurants, cinema, farmers' market, Caltrain. Downhill out, uphill back.
  • Sequoia High School & the El Camino corridor for everyday services.
  • Groceries: none in-neighborhood — nearest supermarkets are a short drive toward downtown / Woodside Plaza.
Bottom line: charming for walks, parklets, and the elementary school, and downtown + a major playground are within a (hilly) mile — but with no stores in the neighborhood and steep streets, it's car-oriented for groceries and daily errands.

7Bottom line

Pros

  • Effectively new: foundation, 200-amp service, plumbing, framing, roof, baths — all permitted and finaled 6/5/2026. Low near-term capex risk.
  • Eagle Hill / Emerald Hills commands a premium over the 94061 flats.
  • Clean permit closure — additions are legal and finaled (no unpermitted-work risk).

Cons / watch-items

  • Top-of-submarket $/sqft (~20% over median); premium rides entirely on condition.
  • Investor renovation — verify build quality, GC/engineer, warranties.
  • Two construction-period complaints (dust, trench shoring).
  • Open sewer-lateral encroachment / ENG hold — confirm closed before COE.
  • Hillside lot + ejector pump + outdoor water heater = ongoing maintenance points.
